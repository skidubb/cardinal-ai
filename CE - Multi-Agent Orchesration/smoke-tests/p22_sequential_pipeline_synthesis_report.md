## Executive Thesis

Most firms treat onboarding as administration. We treat it as the single highest-leverage moment in the entire client lifecycle — a 21-day window that determines whether we become an irreplaceable strategic partner or a replaceable vendor. Every analysis we've conducted confirms this thesis, and every function has contributed to making it executable.

The strategic design establishes what we do and why. The financial architecture ensures we can afford to do it — and only where the economics justify it. The technical systems make execution structurally inevitable rather than dependent on heroics. The experience design ensures the process doesn't just function — it *means something* to the client.

What follows is the integrated plan.

---

## I. The Governing Framework: "The Foundation Sprint"

We never use the word "onboarding" with clients. Our process is called **The Foundation Sprint** — language that signals investment, intentionality, and speed. It gives clients vocabulary to explain internally what's happening ("We're in the Foundation Sprint") and positions the time investment as purposeful rather than bureaucratic.

The Foundation Sprint moves through five phases over 21 days. Each phase has a strategic objective, defined economics, technical enablement, and specific emotional design. Every phase terminates with a decision gate — no phase advances without deliberate approval tracked in our systems.

**Three brand attributes the client should be able to articulate by Day 21, even if they can't name them explicitly:**

| Attribute | How They Experience It |
|---|---|
| **Confident** | "They have clear opinions and aren't afraid to recommend." |
| **Candid** | "They told us things we didn't want to hear — and we're better for it." |
| **Curious** | "They asked questions nobody else has asked us." |
