Critically, this finding is reinforced by the performance of the competitive hypothesis family as a whole: H5 (competitive TCO displacement) and H9 (platform incumbent displacement) also survive at zero inconsistencies, suggesting competitive dynamics are the dominant causal cluster. Equally important is what the matrix disconfirms: the macro-driven hypotheses (H3 and H6), which would implicate market timing and budget paralysis as root causes, accumulate 2 and 3 inconsistencies respectively—meaning the available evidence actively contradicts the narrative that our stall is merely a function of macro spending headwinds. While product-market fit hypotheses (H2, H7) also survive at zero inconsistencies, they carry more neutral evidence items (2 and 4 respectively), indicating weaker positive evidentiary support. The weight of evidence therefore points to a competitive origin—our pipeline is stalling not because the market has turned off, but because the market is turning to alternatives that have closed the differentiation gap.

- The precise mechanism within the competitive displacement cluster remains unresolved: whether displacement is driven by feature parity (H1), TCO restructuring (H5), or platform bundling (H9) has materially different strategic implications for our response, and the current evidence does not differentiate among these three sub-hypotheses.
