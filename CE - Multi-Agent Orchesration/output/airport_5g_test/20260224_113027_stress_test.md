- **[AT&T-V4] DPA Liability Trap — AT&T Bears FCC Compliance Risk Under Governance It Cannot Control**: Partially Resolved — This is the most technically substantive vulnerability in the entire exercise, and both the CRO and Cargo Director correctly acknowledge it as a genuine gap requiring modification. The CRO's proposed DPA Emergency Capacity Protocol is well-designed: graduated capacity thresholds, proportional allocation at Level 2, bright-line carve-out preventing DFW overrides that require FCC non-compliance. The Cargo Director's complementary proposal — design-time DPA capacity engineering requiring the network to deliver all degradation-mode minimums under worst-case sustained DPA — converts the runtime governance problem into a testable engineering specification, which is even better. However, neither modification has been drafted, agreed, or incorporated. The residual risk identified by the CRO — that DFW's entire private 5G value proposition depends on spectrum that is federally interruptible — is fundamental and permanent. CBRS is Priority Access / General Authorized Access spectrum subordinate to Incumbent Access by law. No contractual framework changes this. From my concessions perspective: my 1 Mbps degradation floor during DPA events is achievable within post-DPA capacity if the network is properly engineered with DPA headroom. But the consensus does not currently require that headroom engineering as a binding design specification. That is the gap.
- **[AT&T-V5] CWP Degradation Halt Authority Creates Asymmetric Schedule Weapon Against AT&T**: Partially Resolved — The CRO's proposed modifications — seasonal normalization model, supplementation with AT&T's 5+ years of historical CWP data, joint attribution methodology with independent RF adjudicator, and limiting the 200 bps penalty to AT&T-attributed delays only — are comprehensive and technically sound. DFW infrastructure CapEx seems appropriate since the failover protects DFW's overall network value proposition. From my concessions perspective: the DPA + ground-stop scenario also affects my Tier 4 POS traffic — during this event, POS drops to the 1 Mbps degradation floor or worse. A licensed-spectrum backup for airline ramp operations frees up residual CBRS capacity for my POS locations during DPA events. - **[Airline-V5] Terminal F Competitive Carrier-Operations Evaluation Creates Operational Risk During Greenfield Launch**: Partially Resolved — The CRO's architectural defense is technically correct: DFW owns all infrastructure below the carrier-operations API layer, including SDN controllers and slice management.
