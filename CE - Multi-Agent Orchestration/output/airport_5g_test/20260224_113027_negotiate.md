# 1. NEGOTIATED OUTCOME

## 1.1 Architecture

**Model:** Hybrid Co-Investment — DFW owns all physical and logical infrastructure below a documented carrier-operations API layer; AT&T operates the carrier layer (spectrum management, SAS coordination, radio resource management) under a performance-based services contract. Revenue is generated through multi-tenant access fees, carrier offload agreements, and DFW-exclusive data monetization.

**Ownership Demarcation:**

| Layer | Owner | Operator |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Physical infrastructure (fiber, conduit, small cells, antennas, DAS, mounting hardware, edge compute, switches, routers, firewalls, SDN controllers) | DFW — title and deed | DFW-contracted maintenance; AT&T operates carrier functions on DFW hardware |
| Carrier-operations layer (SAS coordination, DPA response, CBRS channel/power control, inter-slice radio resource scheduling) | DFW (infrastructure); AT&T (operational authority) | AT&T — sole real-time authority within defined envelope |
| Tenant slice policy layer (QoS, ACLs, device provisioning, traffic prioritization) | DFW (infrastructure) | Self-service for airline and cargo via API/portal; DFW-managed for concessions |
| Data and analytics layer | DFW — exclusive commercial rights to Tier 1 data | DFW builds/operates analytics and advertising platforms |

## 1.3 Priority Hierarchy and Time-Sequenced Degradation Model

**Published as Exhibit A to the Master Services Agreement — Binding Technical Specification**

Changes require written consent of all Tier 0 and Tier 1 tenants. Changes to any tier's guaranteed bandwidth floor require consent of the affected tier's tenant representatives.

### Normal Operations Hierarchy

| Tier | Category | Uptime SLA | Latency SLA | Bandwidth Guarantee |
|------|----------|------------|-------------|---------------------|
| 0 | Safety-critical (FAA/TSA mandated, emergency comms) | 99.99% | <10ms | Absolute priority, never shed |
| 1 | Airline ramp operations (pushback, baggage, crew, gates) | 99.99% per terminal | <20ms (10ms for safety-critical subset) | Guaranteed minimum, never shed; 50 Mbps sustained per gate area |
| 2 | Federal inspection (CBP, TSA checkpoint analytics) | 99.95% | <20ms | Guaranteed minimum capacity |
| 3 | Cargo automation (AGV control, cold chain, sort operations) | 99.99% per warehouse zone | <20ms AGV / <100ms cold chain | Guaranteed minimum; ≤0.001% packet loss; ≥99.95% handover; 1,000+ devices/warehouse |
| 4 | Concession POS / Financial transactions | 99.9% per location | <50ms | 5 Mbps down / 2 Mbps up per location (1 Gbps total campus); ≤0.1% packet loss |
| 5 | Airport operations (DFW facility management, building systems) | 99.9% | <100ms | Managed allocation |
| 6 | Commercial IoT, digital signage, advertising, non-POS concession | 99.5% | Best-effort | Best-effort above minimum viable |
| 7 | Passenger Wi-Fi, carrier offload, streaming | Best-effort | Best-effort | First to shed |

### Time-Sequenced Degradation Model (Capacity Crunch Events)

**Trigger:** DPA event, equipment failure affecting >20% zone capacity, or declared network capacity emergency.

| Time Window | Tier 0 (Safety) | Tier 1 (Airline Ramp) | Tier 2 (Federal) | Tier 3 (Cargo) | Tier 4 (POS) | Tier 5 (Airport Ops) | Tier 6 (Commercial IoT) | Tier 7 (Passenger Wi-Fi) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Seconds 0-30** | Full allocation | Full allocation | Full allocation | Maintain min viable | Maintain min viable | Maintain min viable | Maintain min viable | Maintain min viable |
| **Seconds 30-300** | Full allocation | **Full allocation** | Full allocation | AGV ≤50ms (reduced-speed mode); cold chain continuity maintained | **1 Mbps per location floor** | Reduced to min viable | **Fully shed** | **Fully shed** |
| **After 300 seconds (Sustained DPA)** | Full allocation | Full allocation | Proportional reduction | AGV ≤75ms (safe-stop mode); cold chain failover to backup telemetry | 1 Mbps floor maintained | Proportional reduction | Shed | Shed |

**Automated notifications during degradation:**
- 30-second automated alert to cargo operations center when cargo slice is deprioritized (AGVs switch to reduced-speed mode)
- 30-second automated alert to DFW concession support desk when POS traffic enters degradation territory (below 5/2 Mbps)
- 60-minute post-event notification to airline 24/7 NOC and cargo operations center following any zero-notice emergency security directive affecting their slices
